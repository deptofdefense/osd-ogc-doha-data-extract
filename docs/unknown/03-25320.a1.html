The Defense Office of Hearings and Appeals (DOHA) declined to grant Applicant a security clearance. On January 11, 2005, DOHA issued a statement of reasons advising Applicant of the basis for that decision--security concerns raised under Guideline G (Alcohol Consumption) and Guideline E (Personal Conduct) of Department of Defense Directive 5220.6 (Jan. 2, 1992), as amended(Directive). Applicant requested a hearing. On January 31, 2006, after the hering, Administrative Judge Philip S. Howe denied Applicant's request for a security clearance. Applicant timely appealed pursuant to the Directive ¶¶ E3.1.28 and E3.1.30.

Applicant's appeal brief contains no assertion of error on the part of the Administrative Judge. It only contains a request that Applicant be granted a clearance with a warning that any future incidents of a similar nature may result in revocation of access.

The Appeal Board's authority to review a case is limited to cases in which the appealing party has alleged the Administrative Judge committed harmful error. Applicant has not made an allegation of harmful error. Therefore, the decision of the Administrative Judge denying Applicant a security clearance is AFFIRMED.
