The Defense Office of Hearings and Appeals (DOHA) declined to grant Applicant a security clearance. On August 11, 2005, DOHA issued a statement of reasons (SOR) advising Applicant of the basis for that decision--security concerns raised under Guideline F (Financial Considerations) of Department of Defense Directive 5220.6 (Jan. 2, 1992, as amended) (Directive). Applicant requested a hearing. On March 27, 2006, after the hearing, Administrative Judge ichael J. Breslin denied Applicant's request for a security clearance. Applicant timely appealed pursuant to the Directive ¶¶ E3.1.28 and E3.1.30.

Applicant's appeal brief contains no assertion of error on the part of the Administrative Judge. It only contains a request that the Board reconsider the Judge's decision and reinstate

Applicant's clearance in order to give her an opportunity to resolve her financial problems by filing for bankruptcy. (1)

The Board's authority to review a case is limited to cases in which the appealing party has alleged the Administrative Judge committed harmful error. Applicant has not made an allegation of harmful error. The Board does not review a case de novo. It has no authority to grant Applicant a conditional or probationary security clearance to allow her the opportunity to have a security clearance while she works on her financial problems. See ISCR Case No. 03-07418 at 3 (App. Bd. Oct. 13, 2004). Therefore, the decision of the Administrative Judge denying Applicant a security clearance is AFFIRMED.
