The Defense Office of Hearings and Appeals (DOHA) declined to grant Applicant a security clearance. On August 10, 2005, DOHA issued a statement of reasons advising Applicant of the basis for that decision--security concerns raised under Guideline G (Alcohol Consumption) and Guideline J (Criminal Conduct) of Department of Defense Directive 5220.6 (Jan. 2, 1992), as amended (Directive). Applicant requested the case be decided on the written record. On April 10, 2006, after considering the record, Chief Administrative Judge Robert Robinson Gales denied Applicant's request for a security clearance. Applicant timely appealed pursuant to the Directive ¶¶ E3.1.28 and E3.1.30.

Applicant's appeal brief contains no assertion of error on the part of the Chief Administrative Judge. It only contains new evidence, in the form of documents, which the Board cannot consider. See Directive ¶ E3.1.29.

The Appeal Board's authority to review a case is limited to cases in which the appealing party has alleged the Administrative Judge committed harmful error. Applicant has not made an allegation of harmful error. Therefore, the decision of the Chief Administrative Judge denying Applicant a security clearance is AFFIRMED.
